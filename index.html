5-6% global price increases annually without consumer resistance. FY26 guidance: +5-6% pricing + 6-7% volumes. May 2025: extra 4-5% US price hike for tariffs.

Why Aspirational Demand Matters

Morgan Stanley's key headwind #1: middle-income consumer softness. Unlike Chanel or Loro Piana, Hermès has a sizeable gifting/entry-price business (ties, scarves, sandals, jewelry) catering to aspirational customers.

Tracking search volume for "Hermès gift", "Hermès scarf", and "Hermès sandals" provides a real-time proxy for this aspirational demand layer. A decline here while core "Birkin" searches hold is a classic mixed signal.

Key Insight: Pre-Spend Ratio Erosion

Bernstein notes that Birkin resale premiums fell from ~2.2x retail in 2022 to ~1.4x by late 2025. If this continues toward 1.0x, the "quota tax" (mandatory pre-spend on scarves/RTW to qualify for a Birkin allocation) becomes harder to justify, structurally undermining the pre-spend flywheel that drives ~30% of revenue.

Supply Constraint = Feature, Not Bug

Unlike competitors that expand production to capture demand, Hermès deliberately bottlenecks growth through the lengthy timelines required to train new artisans (2 years + 1 year internship). This strict demand-pull model ensures demand > supply, eliminating need for discounting.

Bear risk: Kepler warns that slower-than-expected capacity increases could hold back the most profitable category's growth.

Bull / Bear Thesis Scorecard

Bull Case Arguments

1
Pricing Power: 5-6% annual increases without consumer resistance. No discounting. Zero markdown policy.
2
Scarcity Moat: Demand permanently exceeds supply. Waitlists are a feature. Competitors cannot replicate artisan model.
3
Fortress Balance Sheet: €12.8B net cash. FCF/NI ~90%. Cumulative dividend potential: €13.8B over next 5 years (RBC).
4
New Categories: Haute couture (2027), cosmetics/skincare (2027-28), tech accessories (Kelly headphones). Expanding TAM.
5
Defensive Safe Haven: Highest sales/store in industry. Structural demand for leather goods. 21% TSR over 20 years.

Bear Case Arguments

1
Valuation Extreme: 47x forward P/E. If growth slows to MSD, rapid de-rating risk (CFRA). Two-thirds of equity value in terminal value.
2
ME Headwind: -40% offshore spending Feb-Mar 2026. Geopolitical disruption to traffic-driven categories and Eid spending.
3
China Deceleration: APAC ex-JP from +7% to +5%. Aspirational consumer still challenged. Lower local price increases in FY26.
4
Margin Unwind Risk: Post-COVID margins may prove unsustainable. Multi-year unwind not in consensus (RBC).
5
Product Concentration: Birkin & Kelly ~25-33% of sales/profit. Exotic skin sourcing ESG risk. California tying lawsuit.
